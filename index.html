About Nairobi

Situated in the heart of Kenya, Nairobi is an amazing city with fantastic attractions like Nairobi National Park, the David Sheldrick Wildlife Trust, the Karen Blixen Museum, and more. The people of Nairobi primarily speak English and Swahili, and the city is a key economic hub in the East Africa region.

A city of contrasts, Nairobi blends modern skyscrapers with rich natural landscapes. It's a place where diverse cultures and traditions converge, offering significant experiences like wildlife safaris, vibrant markets, and historical landmarks. Visitors can explore the bustling central business district, the serene Karura Forest, and the cultural Bomas of Kenya, all while enjoying the dynamic energy of a rapidly growing city.

Popular Places in Nairobi

Nairobi Expressway

The Nairobi Expressway is a major infrastructure project in Kenya designed to alleviate traffic congestion in Nairobi and improve connectivity in the region. Stretching 27 kilometers from Mlolongo to Westlands, the expressway runs through key areas, including Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA) and the Nairobi Central Business District, making it an essential transit corridor.

Global Trade Centre(GTC)

The Global Trade Centre (GTC) in Nairobi is a landmark mixed-use development located in the Westlands area. Completed in 2022, the GTC comprises six towers, including the 184-meter GTC Office Tower, which is the tallest building in East Africa. The project cost approximately KES 52 billion and incorporates a combination of office spaces, luxury apartments, a hotel, and a shopping mall, making it a hub for business, living, and leisure.

Britam Tower

Britam Tower, located in Upper Hill, Nairobi, is Kenya's tallest building and one of the most iconic landmarks in the city. Standing at 200.1 meters, it comprises 32 usable floors, making it a prominent feature in the Nairobi skyline. The building's unique design features a prismatic shape, widening at the base and tapering as it rises, which gives it a striking appearance. It was designed by GAPP Architects and Urban Designers in collaboration with Triad Architects and completed in 2017 at a cost of KSh 8.8 billion

Cooperative Bank

Cooperative Bank House, is a notable landmark in Nairobi, Kenya. Completed in 1981, this 25-story skyscraper is located on Haile Selassie Avenue and serves as the headquarters for the Cooperative Bank of Kenya. Standing 83 meters (272 feet) tall, it is primarily used as an office space and showcases a solid yet elegant architectural design by Swiss architects. Its distinct structure contributes to Nairobi’s skyline and reflects Kenya’s growing prominence in commercial and financial sectors

Security

Kenya has invested significantly in improving its security infrastructure, including advanced surveillance systems and modernized law enforcement. These efforts have enhanced the safety of urban areas and key economic zones​. Despite occasional issues, popular destinations such as national parks, the Rift Valley, and coastal beaches remain safe and well-secured for both locals and visitors. These areas contribute to Kenya's global reputation as a leading safari and ecotourism destination​.

KICC(Kenyatta International Convention Centre)

The Kenyatta International Convention Centre (KICC) is a renowned landmark in Nairobi, Kenya. Completed in 1973, it is a 28-story structure primarily used for conferences, exhibitions, and events. The building features a unique cylindrical design and a rooftop viewing deck that offers panoramic views of Nairobi and its surroundings. Standing at 105 meters, it was once the tallest building in Kenya and remains a symbol of the country's progress and modernity.

Current Situation (Kenya)

Kenya is currently navigating a mix of opportunities and challenges as it enters 2025. The economy has shown resilience, with significant improvements in key indicators. Inflation has dropped to its lowest in decades, the Kenyan shilling has strengthened, and foreign exchange reserves have increased, offering economic stability.
